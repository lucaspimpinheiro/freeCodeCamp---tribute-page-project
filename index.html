Dom Pedro II of Brazil: "the Magnanimous"

The last Emperor of Brazil, a man who loved his country.

A timeline of Pedro II's life:

  • 1825 - Born in Rio de Janeiro - Brazil
  • 1831 - His father abdicates and left to Europe, leaving Pedro II obliged to study in preparation for rule
  • 1841 - Pedro II is coronated in the Imperial Chapel in Rio
  • 1843 - Marries Teresa Cristina of the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies, the ceremony took place in Naples, Italy
  • 1845 - The Emperor made a tour of Brazil's southern provinces, enthusiasticaly received by his people
  • 1848 - While the Emperor confronted the trade of illegally imported slaves, Praieira revolt erupted in Northeastern Brazil, suppressed only a year later
  • 1850 - The Eusebio de Queiros Law was promulgated, which gave the Brazilian Empire broad authority to combat illegal slave trade.
  • 1852 - The Emperor has forged an alliance between Brazil, Uruguay and disaffected Argentines, leading to the Platine war, and subsequent overthrown of the Argentine dictator and stabilization of the region
  • 1859 - Pedro II departed on a trip to provinces north of the capital, visiting Espírito Santo, Bahia, Sergipe, Alagoas, Pernambuco and Paraíba. The trip was a huge success, with the Emperor welcomed everywhere with warmth and joy
  • 1862 - British consul in Rio nearly sparkled a war, as he sent an ultimatum containing abusive demands out of a minor incident, the arrest of drunken British officers who were causing a disturbance in the streets of Rio. The Emperor refused to yield. Even after the consul issued orders for British warships to capture Brazilian merchan vessels as indemnity. The consul then proposed a peaceful settlement through international arbitration. The Brazilian government presented its demands and, upon seeing the British government's position weaken, severed diplomatic ties with Britain
  • 1864 - Under the dictator Solano Lopez, Paraguay invaded Brazil and Argentina, triggering the Paraguayan War. Pedro II decides to go to the front in person. Pedro II's coordination of the military operations and his personal example played a decisive role in successfully repulsing the Paraguayan invasion of Brazilian territory. Before returning to Rio de Janeiro, he received the British diplomatic envoy Edward Thornton, who apologized on behalf of Queen Victoria and the British Government for the crisis between the empires. The Emperor regarded this diplomatic victory over the most powerful nation of the world as sufficient and renewed friendly relations.
  • 1870 - The Paraguayan war ends with a Brazilian victory
  • 1871 - few Brazilians opposed slavery and even fewer openly condemned it. Pedro II, who did not own slaves, was one of the few who did oppose slavery. He consciously ignored the growing political damage to his image and to the monarchy in consequence of his support for abolition. in 1871, was enacted the Law of Free Birth, under which all children born to slave women after that date were considered free-born.
  • 1872 - Tour through Europe and North Africa: The Emperor proceeded to visit Spain, Great Britain, Belgium, Germany, Austria, Italy, Egypt, Greece, Switzerland and France. In Coburg he visited his daughter's tomb. He traveled under the assumed name "Dom Pedro de Alcântara", insisting upon being treated informally and staying only in hotels.
  • 1889 - Although there was no desire in Brazil among the majority of the population to change the form of government, the military launched a coup d'état, overthrowing the monarchy. Pedro II dismissed all suggestions for quelling the rebellion that politicians and military leaders put forward to avoid a civil war that would only bring suffering to his people. So he and his family were sent into exile in Europe
  • 1891 - Death of Pedro II of Brazil

"May god grant me these last wishes: Peace and Prosperity for Brazil" -- Pedro II of Brazil
